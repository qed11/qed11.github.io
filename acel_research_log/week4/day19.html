Day 19 - June 3, 2022

Attended a mock lecture given by Dr. Philipp Seiler on Signed Distance Function. A very cool idea about implicitly representing an enclosed object. $\renewcommand\vec{\mathbf} $
  • When we have an enclosed domain $\Omega \in \mathbb{R}^n$, we can find a function \(f: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}\) such that $$ \begin{cases} f(\vec{x}) < 0, \quad \vec{x} \in \Omega \\ f(\vec{x}) > 0, \quad \vec{x} \notin \Omega \\ f(\vec{x}) = 0, \quad \vec{x} \in \partial \Omega \\ \end{cases} $$
  • This representation allows us to define faces with simple equations, which then allows us to construct complex shapes with basic arithmetics. Between two domains $\Omega_1 \: \text{and} \: \Omega_2$ with implicit function $\phi_1 \: \text{and} \: \phi_2$, we can have $$\text{Union:} \: \Omega_1 \cup \Omega_2 = \min{(\phi_1, \phi_2)}$$ $$\text{Intersection:} \: \Omega_1 \cap \Omega_2 = \max{(\phi_1, \phi_2)}$$
    • A signed distance function is a special type of such implicit functions, where it is defined as the shortest distance to the boundary of the shape. A negative values indicates that it is inside the shape, and a positive value is vice versa.
